STAR SKIMMER

A p5.js game filled with stars by Alex Hopkins

Instructions:

Use the mouse or touchscreen to control the ship by hovering the cursor over stars to move towards them.
Follow the arrow to reach the goal, it will become more red as you get closer.
You can collect boost powerups to speed up your ship but becareful to avoid flying into them at a difficult angle as they can throw you off course!

About the design:

The theme I was going for is one of the wonder and endless expanse of the starry night sky. Before I started I had envisioned thousands of twinkling stars and wanted to create a game involving them in the gameplay.
The mechanics began as an idea for controlling a ship through the sky and avoiding the objects, like many top down 2d games. However as I began coding I decided to try limiting the control by having the ship only be able to grapple to stars for it's movement. I ended up spending a lot of time creating the visuals using p5 shapes for a more minimal and primal feel.
I decided to use a website called beepbox to create the sounds since it was very good at making 8bit sounds. I was particularly pleased with the soundtrack and visual change in ambience that plays when you hit a shooting star, it was very rewarding to see my friends reactions when they first discovered that mechanic.
The second mode of the game is one used in many racing games, where the standard gameplay is intensified by a time limit. I worked with this by adding a faster soundtrack with a more red colour palette, since red is often associated with danger.
